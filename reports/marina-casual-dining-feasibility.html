Marina Casual Dining — Year 1 Model

Client Brief

The ask

The client was evaluating a high-footfall marina site with a known-operator tenant next door. They needed a lender-ready feasibility pack within 5 working days to respond to the landlord's RFP.

Problem

Landlord RFP in 5 days

Location was competitive, rent was above average, landlord wanted a concept story plus hard numbers — not a generic pitch deck. Existing 2019 spreadsheet was stale.

Opportunity

Proven marina footfall

Evening & weekend footfall in the corridor was benchmarked at 45k+ visitors/week. Strong spend-per-head data for adjacent casual dining tenants made the upside credible.

🔒
Fully sanitized. Client name, store name, exact locations & trading figures have been scrubbed. Industry, scale, deal structure & feasibility framework are preserved so you can see exactly how the work is shaped.
Approach

How we built it

A 5-step repeatable process — from brief to investor-ready pack, powered by AI across financial modelling, market sizing & narrative writing.

Site & traffic benchmarking

Pulled publicly-available footfall data for the corridor, cross-checked against 3 comparable marina casual-dining tenants.

Menu engineering & price points

Built a 60-item menu with GP per item, targeting 32% blended GP. Tested against 3 comparable menus in the district.

CapEx & fit-out cost model

Broken into 9 heads — civil, MEP, kitchen equipment, FF&E, branding, licensing, soft opening, working capital, contingency.

Month-by-month P&L & cash flow

Year 1 monthly — revenue build, COGS, staff ramp, rent, utilities, marketing, D&A. Year 2 & 3 quarterly summary.

Investor pack & lender memo

One-page exec summary, 12-slide investor deck, full Excel model with editable assumption tab, and a 2-page lender memo.

Outcome

What the client walked away with

Client submitted the pack inside the 5-day window. The landlord shortlisted them against two competing operators. Final decision still in negotiation at time of writing.

Signal

Shortlisted on first submission

Pack was strong enough to get past the landlord's initial filter — the only operator from Dubai Marina area that included a lender-ready P&L.

Signal

Re-usable model

Same spreadsheet has since been re-purposed by the client to evaluate two other marina sites — editable assumption tab was the key selling point.
