Urban Lifestyle — D2C Setup

An urban-apparel brand migrating from wholesale-dominant to D2C-dominant — platform, logistics, marketing & ops blueprint.

Client Brief

The ask

An apparel brand with 7 years of wholesale history wanted to shift 40%+ of revenue to D2C within 12 months — without cannibalising their retail stockists.

Problem

Wholesale vs D2C channel conflict

Stockists were nervous. Pricing arbitrage, stock allocation and launch exclusivity all needed thinking — the setup wasn't just tech, it was channel diplomacy.

Opportunity

Known brand, weak online

Brand awareness was already strong through wholesale. Organic traffic was being captured by stockists' sites — a direct site could recapture 30%+ of that demand.

Approach

How we built it

A 5-step repeatable process — from brief to investor-ready pack, powered by AI across financial modelling, market sizing & narrative writing.

Platform selection & stack

Shopify Plus vs Salesforce B2C vs custom. Shopify Plus won on speed-to-launch and ecosystem maturity in the GCC.

Stockist protection & pricing policy

MAP policy, early-drop exclusivity, online-only SKU tier. Designed to grow D2C without blowing up wholesale.

Logistics & returns operations

Two 3PL partners (UAE + KSA), returns SLA of 5 days, reverse logistics flow. SLA-level contract template drafted.

Year 1 marketing plan & CAC ladder

Meta/Google at the top, TikTok creator seeding mid-funnel, email/SMS at the bottom. CAC ladder tuned by customer segment.

Implementation roadmap

9-month Gantt — platform build (month 1-3), soft launch (4-5), scale (6-9). Weekly checkpoint template for the internal PM.

What the client walked away with

Platform went live at month 4. D2C share reached 38% by month 14 — slightly below target but ahead of industry benchmarks. No major stockist lost.
