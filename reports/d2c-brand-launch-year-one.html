Client Brief

The ask

A first-time D2C founder had a product, a supplier and a Shopify theme. Needed a Year 1 projection that would survive scrutiny from angel investors — meaning real CAC math, not hockey-stick fantasy.

Problem

Ambitious, but un-modelled

Founder was running AED 300 Meta tests with a AED 280 AOV. Math didn't work. Needed to see it on paper before raising.

Opportunity

Marketplace cross-over

D2C alone was tough economics. Amazon.ae + Noon cross-over with 15-20% marketplace revenue share changed the blended unit economics materially.

🔒
Fully sanitized. Client name, store name, exact locations & trading figures have been scrubbed. Industry, scale, deal structure & feasibility framework are preserved so you can see exactly how the work is shaped.
Approach

How we built it

A 5-step repeatable process — from brief to investor-ready pack, powered by AI across financial modelling, market sizing & narrative writing.

Product catalogue & AOV tiers

180 SKU across 4 AOV tiers (AED 180 / 280 / 420 / 650). Bundle strategy designed to lift AOV by 18% via cart upsells.

CAC & channel mix model

Meta/TikTok/Google Performance Max, each with its own CAC curve. Retargeting layer driving 22% of revenue at 60% lower CAC.

Marketplace cross-over economics

Amazon.ae + Noon at 20% of revenue but 22% margin take after fees & FBA. Reduces blended margin but improves cashflow stability.

Year 1 monthly P&L

GMV, COGS, ad spend, fulfilment, returns reserve — monthly. Break-even at month 9, cash-positive month 11.

Angel pitch & data-room setup

12-slide pitch, full Excel model with live Shopify export, plus a data-room template the founder could populate and send to angels directly.
