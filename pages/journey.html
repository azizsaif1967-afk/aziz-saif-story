The Arc

Retail, franchise, and the world.

Chapter One

Dubai, at eighteen

The first flight. The first job. The first boss. Sana Fashions opened its door to a teenager from the subcontinent — and the next thirty-seven years of retail, family, and Gulf business all started there.

  • Arrived 1989 — joined Sana Fashions as one of the first employees
  • Learned the floor — from stockroom to store manager before the first promotion
  • Grew with the brand — one store, then five, then thirty-five
  • Made Dubai home — family, community, and a career anchored in the UAE

Chapter Two

GCC — home turf

The Gulf is home. Stores opened, payrolls run, malls walked, landlords negotiated, suppliers chased — all across five markets that each behave differently.

UAEOmanKSAKuwaitIraq
UAE · Home baseUnited Arab Emirates
Pop. ~10.2MCapital: Abu Dhabi

Unique: A federation of seven emirates born in 1971 — home to the world's tallest tower, Burj Khalifa.

Home base since 1989. Landed in Dubai with a suitcase, grew up inside a 35-store retail company, raised a family, and learned the rhythm of the Gulf.

Oman · Le Pearl countrySultanate of Oman
Pop. ~4.6MCapital: Muscat

Unique: One of the oldest independent states in the Arab world — frankincense country for 3,000+ years.

Opened Le Pearl flagship stores across Muscat & Salalah — the quietest, most loyal GCC customers I've ever served.

KSA · Master franchiseKingdom of Saudi Arabia
Pop. ~36.9MCapital: Riyadh

Unique: Holds the two holiest cities of Islam — Mecca & Madinah — and the world's largest oil reserves.

Negotiated the YOYOSO master-franchise rights — the biggest single deal of my career. Riyadh-Jeddah-Dammam on rotation for months.

Kuwait · Tough marketState of Kuwait
Pop. ~4.3MCapital: Kuwait City

Unique: Highest-valued currency unit in the world — 1 Kuwaiti Dinar is worth more than any other currency.

Retail expansion into a notoriously tough mall landscape — demanding customers, excellent payers, sharp negotiators.

Bahrain · Cross-causewayKingdom of Bahrain
Pop. ~1.5MCapital: Manama

Unique: The first Gulf state to discover oil (1932) — also the smallest country in the Arab world.

Quiet market visits and cross-causeway sourcing — Bahrain was always the friendly stop between KSA and the UAE.

Qatar · Premium retailState of Qatar
Pop. ~2.9MCapital: Doha

Unique: Hosted the first FIFA World Cup in the Arab world (2022) — among the highest GDP per capita on earth.

Doha trade-fair runs and supplier meetings — small population, serious spending power, premium-only retail.

Iraq · ResilienceRepublic of Iraq
Pop. ~45.5MCapital: Baghdad

Unique: The cradle of civilisation — Mesopotamia, the birthplace of writing, law, and the wheel.

Post-recovery retail visits — raw energy, resilient customers, and a reminder that commerce returns the moment peace does.

03
Chapter Three

Subcontinent — sourcing roots

Where fabric, factories, and family intersect. The Subcontinent is where generations of trading knowledge live — and where a lot of the apparel on Gulf shelves is still cut and stitched.

India · RootsRepublic of India
Pop. ~1.43BCapital: New Delhi

Unique: The most populous country on earth — and the birthplace of the number zero, chess, and yoga.

Where the trading path started — Mumbai buying offices, Delhi weddings, Surat fabric, and family all at once. Roots, always.

Bangladesh · CapacityPeople's Republic of Bangladesh
Pop. ~173MCapital: Dhaka

Unique: The world's second-largest garment exporter — a single country clothes a significant share of the planet.

Dhaka factory visits where I learned what "capacity" really means — millions of pieces a week, and friendships that outlasted orders.

Sri Lanka · Ceylon tradeDemocratic Socialist Republic of Sri Lanka
Pop. ~22MCapital: Sri Jayawardenepura Kotte

Unique: The pearl of the Indian Ocean — Ceylon tea, cinnamon, and among the oldest continuously-documented histories in Asia.

Colombo sourcing runs, tea gardens in the hills, and family beach weekends — work and wonder on the same trip.

04
Chapter Four

South-East Asia — the sourcing belt

The factories of Vietnam, the showrooms of Thailand, the manufacturing hubs of Malaysia and Indonesia. SE Asia is where a retail operator learns what scale really looks like.

Thailand · MerchandisingKingdom of Thailand
Pop. ~71MCapital: Bangkok

Unique: The only South-East Asian country never colonised by a European power — its name means "land of the free".

Bangkok showrooms, Chatuchak weekend runs, and lifestyle-brand scouting — Thailand taught me how merchandising meets hospitality.

Vietnam · ScaleSocialist Republic of Vietnam
Pop. ~100MCapital: Hanoi

Unique: Has the world's longest cave (Son Doong) — and one of the fastest-growing garment & footwear export industries anywhere.

Factory tours in Ho Chi Minh — a generation of workers turning out footwear & garments at a pace that redefined my idea of "scale".

Malaysia · Halal hubMalaysia
Pop. ~34MCapital: Kuala Lumpur

Unique: Home to the Petronas Twin Towers — for six years, the tallest buildings in the world.

Kuala Lumpur was always the halal-friendly stopover — suppliers, Islamic-finance banking conversations, and the best nasi lemak on earth.

Indonesia · ManufacturingRepublic of Indonesia
Pop. ~277MCapital: Jakarta (moving to Nusantara)

Unique: The world's largest archipelago — over 17,000 islands — and the largest Muslim-majority country on earth.

Jakarta's scale is a separate universe. Manufacturing muscle, spice markets, and the warmest client dinners in the region.

Singapore · Transit hubRepublic of Singapore
Pop. ~6.0MCapital: Singapore

Unique: A city-state that went from third-world to first in a single generation — one of the cleanest, most efficient places on earth.

The transit hub for every Asian sourcing run — Changi arrivals lounge was practically a second home. Every supply chain passes through.

Cambodia · Rising tierKingdom of Cambodia
Pop. ~17MCapital: Phnom Penh

Unique: Home to Angkor Wat — the largest religious monument in the world, built in the 12th century.

Phnom Penh factory runs — the rising-tier sourcing base after Vietnam got expensive. Early-morning floor walks, late-night negotiations.

05
Chapter Five

Greater Asia — corners and crossroads

From the Caucasus to the Levant to the edge of China, the wider Asia map is where cultures, kitchens, and trade routes cross. Each stop added a supplier, a story, or a friend.

Azerbaijan · Land of FireRepublic of Azerbaijan
Pop. ~10.3MCapital: Baku

Unique: The "Land of Fire" — natural gas vents have burned for millennia at Yanar Dag, and the Caspian is the world's largest inland body of water.

Baku surprised me — a Caucasus city with Gulf-level ambition, old-town alleys, and a future-facing waterfront side by side.

Macau · East meets WestMacau (SAR, China)
Pop. ~720KCapital: (SAR of China)

Unique: The only place in China where casinos are legal — and a 400-year Portuguese legacy still in the food, the tiles, and the churches.

A short stop — Portuguese egg tarts, neon facades, and a reminder that East & West have been trading longer than most histories admit.

Turkey · Home goods HQRepublic of Türkiye
Pop. ~85MCapital: Ankara

Unique: The only country that straddles two continents — and Istanbul is the only city in the world spanning Europe and Asia.

Istanbul was the home-goods headquarters — textiles, kitchenware, and every category Le Pearl and YOYOSO ever needed. Grand Bazaar visits always ran late.

Lebanon · Levantine tasteLebanese Republic
Pop. ~5.4MCapital: Beirut

Unique: Home to Byblos — one of the oldest continuously-inhabited cities in the world — and the cedar tree on the flag is mentioned in the Bible.

Beirut — Levantine taste at its most refined. Fashion, food, and business conversations that circled back to the Gulf by dessert.

Syria · Old DamascusSyrian Arab Republic
Pop. ~23MCapital: Damascus

Unique: Damascus is often called the oldest continuously-inhabited capital on earth — 11,000 years and counting.

Old Damascus souks — brass, silk, soap, and stories in every shop. A visit that belongs to a gentler era.

06
Chapter Six

Europe — fashion & shows

Milan fabrics, Barcelona collections, Athens sights, Malta harbours, and the Vatican pilgrimage. Europe was where sourcing trips and cultural trips blurred.

Italy · Milan WeekItalian Republic
Pop. ~59MCapital: Rome

Unique: Home to more UNESCO World Heritage sites than any other country — and the birthplace of the modern bank.

Milan Fashion Week, Florence showrooms, Rome weekends — Italy is where sourcing trips and family holidays permanently blurred.

Spain · Retail inspirationKingdom of Spain
Pop. ~48MCapital: Madrid

Unique: The world's second most-visited country — and the origin of the world's most copied retail playbook (Zara / Inditex).

Barcelona — my retail-inspiration city. Every visit, I came home with a notebook full of merchandising ideas the Gulf hadn't seen yet.

Greece · Cultural stopHellenic Republic
Pop. ~10.4MCapital: Athens

Unique: The birthplace of democracy, the Olympic Games, and Western philosophy — with 6,000 islands scattered across the Aegean.

Athens — where history and the Mediterranean genuinely meet. A slower trip, a cultural one, by design.

Malta · MicrostateRepublic of Malta
Pop. ~540KCapital: Valletta

Unique: One of the smallest EU states — with Neolithic temples older than the pyramids and its own ancient language.

A Mediterranean microstate with big hospitality. Valletta's honey-coloured limestone is unlike anywhere else on earth.

Vatican · Smallest stateVatican City State
Pop. ~500Capital: Vatican City

Unique: The smallest country on earth — 0.49 km² — and the only country with Latin as an official language.

A quieter visit. The smallest country on the list, with some of the biggest history — the Sistine Chapel, seen once, never forgotten.

07
Chapter Seven

North America — the next chapter

Canada is on the plan. The US visit was about learning — observing retail, tech, and startup culture first-hand. A foundation for the next phase of Aziz's work.

Canada · The planCanada
Pop. ~40MCapital: Ottawa

Unique: The world's second-largest country by area — with more lakes than the rest of the world combined.

Canada is the plan. Startup-program exploration, family visits, and the future anchor for building outside the Gulf.

USA · AI originUnited States of America
Pop. ~335MCapital: Washington, D.C.

Unique: The world's largest economy by nominal GDP — and home to Silicon Valley, where most of today's AI tools were born.

Retail, tech, and family — one trip at a time. The AI wave I ride today started there; each visit is equal parts learning and listening.

08
Chapter Eight

Mecca & Madinah — the pilgrimage chapter

Not on a map of trips — a map of life. Umrah and Hajj, the quietest and most important visits. Separate from business, but the backbone under all of it.

  • Mecca — the heart of the year
  • Madinah — the peace of the Prophet's city
  • Repeat visits — not a tick-list; a recurring return
